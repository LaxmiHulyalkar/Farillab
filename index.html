Publications & Research

Research & ICP Innovation

Peer-reviewed publication, active peer reviewer, and a novel patient-specific workflow combining 3D printing with computational fit validation.

Manuscript Under Review · 2025
Reverse Engineering Developmental Defects: A Globally-Accessible Digital Approach for Nasoalveolar Molding in Neonatal Unilateral Cleft Lip and Palate
Genovesi, E.; Canallatos, J.; Atalla, D.; Hulyalkar, L.; Markiewicz, M.; Arany, P. — Conference poster presented, 2024
Validated a fully digital workflow for fabricating Nasoalveolar Molding (NAM) plates using open-source CAD and 3D printing for neonates with unilateral cleft lip and palate. Surface accuracy assessment (n=34, p>0.05) demonstrated no significant difference between digital and traditional methods. FEA confirmed improved flexural strength in 3D-printed NAMs. Work focuses on improving global accessibility, cost-efficiency, and clinical scalability of craniofacial prosthetic fabrication.
